Kagga 101 – Verse #47

"What if everything I see everywhere is fleeting?"

This line introduces the theme of impermanence, encouraging the reader to reflect on the transitory nature of all things in the world.

"To see those hypnotizing images is vision’s duty."

Here, vision is seen as a tool that fulfills its purpose by observing the world—even if what it sees is temporary. The line hints at the enchanting, even deceptive, quality of surface appearances.

"Beholding and experiencing is the path to reality,"

This line affirms that through perception and experience, we approach the essence of truth. Engagement with the world is not an illusion to escape but a necessary path to deeper understanding.

"Which is permanent, reached through the transient!"

This line expresses a powerful paradox: the eternal truth is accessed by embracing the fleeting. It underscores the idea that only by fully living and observing the transient can one discover the timeless.

Interpretation:

The verse urges us to value life’s fleeting moments as they are the gateway to deeper truths. It suggests that the transitory is not separate from the eternal, but rather the bridge to it.

Spiritual Reflection:

This reflects teachings from Buddhism and Vedanta, where impermanence is not denied but embraced as the path to realization. Through awareness of change, we grow toward what never changes.

Philosophical Insight:

By acknowledging the transient, we find access to the permanent. Philosophies that explore non-duality and illusion (Maya) echo this truth—revealing that what is fleeting reveals what is true.

Conclusion:

Ultimately, this verse teaches us not to fear impermanence but to see it as a sacred vehicle to wisdom. It invites us to behold, feel, and live through all of life, for it is there the eternal is found.
