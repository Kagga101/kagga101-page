Kagga 101 – Verse #17

This verse reflects on the nature of life as a game, focusing on the process rather than the outcome. It encourages a mindset that values the experience and journey over the desire to control or predict the results. The verse taps into existential and philosophical themes of acceptance, present moment awareness, and the flow of life.

"Isn't taste of anticipation the prize of this game?"

The opening line highlights the anticipation itself as a central, valuable part of the experience. In life, much like in a game, we often get caught up in the excitement of what’s to come. This line invites us to savor the journey rather than obsess over the end result, reminding us that waiting and wondering can be deeply enriching.

"Will the game progress if the right card doesn’t show for play?"

This metaphor suggests that life, like a game, includes elements of chance. We may wait for perfect conditions or ideal opportunities, but life goes on regardless. The verse encourages us not to get stuck in expectations, but to adapt and play with the hand we’re dealt.

"What’s the point of asking, 'Who’s winning?' or 'Who’s in pain?'"

By challenging these questions, the verse critiques our obsession with comparison and judgment. Winning and losing, success and suffering—these are transient states. The verse asks us to move beyond these dualities and embrace the experience of being alive, without constant measurement.

"When it is the sheer flow of the game that’s the real gain!"

In its conclusion, the verse reminds us that the true reward is found in fully engaging with life. The “flow” is a state of presence and immersion, where we lose ourselves in the moment and find joy not in outcomes, but in participation. Life’s richness lies in its unfolding, not its finish lines.

Overall Interpretation:

This verse is an invitation to let go of rigid expectations and comparisons. It speaks to the beauty of process over product, the value of presence over results. Like a game, life is meant to be played, explored, and experienced—not won. In embracing the flow, we find fulfillment beyond success or struggle.
